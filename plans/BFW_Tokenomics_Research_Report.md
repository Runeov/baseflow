## 2. DeFi Mechanisms for Yield and ROI

### 2.1 Liquidity Mining
Users deposit token pairs into a DEX pool (e.g., BFW/USDC) and receive LP tokens. They stake LP tokens to earn BFW emissions on top of trading fee revenue.

- **Typical APY range:** 15–80% (new protocols), 5–20% (mature protocols)
- **Risk:** Impermanent loss if BFW/USDC price ratio shifts significantly
- **Best use for BFW:** Launch phase incentive — flood initial DEX with deep liquidity by offering high BFW emissions to early LPs

### 2.2 Staking (PoS Validator / Single-Asset)
Lock BFW into a staking contract to earn block rewards or protocol fees.

- **Pure PoS staking APY:** 4–15% depending on participation rate
- **BFW Proof of Green Stake bonus:** Solar-certified nodes earn an additional 1.5–3x multiplier
- **Risk:** Smart contract risk; slashing risk for validators
- **Best use for BFW:** Core long-term yield mechanism replacing inflationary emissions over time

### 2.3 Yield Farming (Multi-Protocol Composability)
Stack multiple DeFi protocols — stake BFW, receive receipt token, deposit in another protocol, earn additional yield.

- **Typical APY range:** 20–200% (early phase), 8–25% (mature)
- **Risk:** Elevated smart contract risk, liquidation risk if leveraged
- **Best use for BFW:** Allow third-party protocols to integrate BFW receipt tokens (stBFW) as collateral

### 2.4 ve-Tokenomics (Vote-Escrow)
Lock BFW for veBFW to earn:
1. Boosted emissions on liquidity pools
2. Protocol revenue share (trading fees, AI inference fees)
3. Governance votes directing where BFW emissions flow

- **Typical APY range:** 8–40% (revenue-backed component), plus boosted emissions
- **Lock periods:** 1 week to 4 years, with proportional veBFW balance
- **Alignment:** 4-year lockers are the protocol's most loyal stakeholders and should be rewarded accordingly

### 2.5 Real Yield (Revenue-Backed Distribution)
BFW earns protocol revenue from:
- DEX trading fees (0.2–0.3% per swap)
- AI inference marketplace fees (per-query in BFW)
- Solar node certification fees
- Bridge fees (cross-chain BFW transfers)

This revenue is distributed in stablecoins (USDC/USDT) or ETH to veBFW holders — not in inflated BFW. This is the model sophisticated investors prefer in 2026.

## 6. Real Yield in DeFi

### 6.1 Definition

**Real yield = Protocol revenue distributed to token holders, denominated in assets other than the protocol's own inflationary token (e.g., USDC, ETH, BTC).**

The distinction:

| Type | Source | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Inflationary Yield | New tokens minted from thin air | Old-era yield farming: 1000% APY in the project's own token |
| Real Yield | Actual protocol fee revenue | GMX distributing 30% of trading fees in ETH to stakers |

### 6.2 Why Inflationary Yields Fail Long-Term

- Supply expands → price dilutes → yield in USD terms collapses
- Relies on new buyers absorbing sell pressure from reward recipients
- "Ponzinomics": yield exists only as long as TVL grows
- Classic example: OlympusDAO (OHM) — 8000% APY collapsed when buyer demand dried up

### 6.3 Why Real Yield Wins in 2025/2026

1. **Institutional grade:** Institutions model yield from revenue, not inflation. Real yield can be underwritten like a dividend.
2. **Sustainability:** Revenue scales with usage, not token price.
3. **Reflexivity is positive:** More users → more revenue → higher yield → more stakers → higher token demand → higher price.
4. **Regulatory clarity:** Revenue-based distributions are easier to model for compliance than inflationary emissions.
5. **Market evidence:** In 2025, share of DeFi fees reaching token holders tripled from 5% to 15%. Protocols leading this shift (GMX, Hyperliquid, Aave) outperformed peers.

### 6.4 Real Yield Examples (Confirmed Protocols)

### 9.4 Token Velocity

**Definition:** How frequently the total token supply changes hands. Measured as:
> Velocity = Annual Transaction Volume ÷ Average Market Cap

**Why it matters:** High velocity = tokens are used but not held = price pressure. Low velocity = tokens are hoarded/staked = bullish supply constraint.

**Good tokenomics creates velocity sinks:**
- Staking locks tokens (reduces velocity)
- veBFW lock periods (4 years = near-zero velocity)
- LP positions lock tokens
- AI inference payment burn (removes tokens from circulation)

**Industry target:** Velocity < 5 for a healthy token economy. Governance tokens like UNI/AAVE have velocity < 1 due to long-term holding culture.
