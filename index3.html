Emergency Network · Technical

How Crisis Broadcast Works

Step 1 — Detect
Node monitors grid voltage + cellular signal. Sub-2 second failover trigger. Can also be activated manually via app or Govt API push.
Step 2 — Switch
GPU compute workloads pause. Battery and solar redirect to Wi-Fi AP + Starlink relay. Mesh module activates — connects to neighbouring nodes automatically.
Step 3 — Broadcast
Open SSID published. Local AI model runs offline on node for emergency Q&A and translation. Live data pushed via Starlink to national emergency ops.
  • Hardware cost per node: Add ~$150–300 for TP-Link mesh radio module + antenna — less than 2% of total node cost
  • Target regions: Thailand typhoon corridor, Philippines, Bangladesh, coastal Indonesia, East Africa — UN-estimated 3.5B people at risk from climate events
  • Business model: Annual retainer contracts from NDMO + UN agencies (~$500–2,000/node/year at scale)
  • Differentiation: No other AI compute network has a built-in crisis infrastructure mandate — makes Baseflow a critical national asset, not just a tech startup
