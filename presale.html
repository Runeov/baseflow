Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
When do I receive my SAI tokens? ▼
SAI tokens are distributed at the Token Generation Event (TGE). Your purchase is recorded on-chain immediately, and tokens will be airdropped directly to the wallet address you used for purchase. You'll receive a notification when TGE is scheduled.
What network does this run on? ▼
The presale runs on Polygon Mainnet (chainId 137). You'll need USDT on Polygon and a small amount of MATIC for gas fees (~0.01 MATIC per transaction). If you're on the wrong network, the page will prompt you to switch.
Is SAI a security or investment product? ▼
No. SAI is a utility token that powers the Baseflow solar-AI node network. It is used for node operations, AI compute access, and network governance. This presale is not an offering of securities. Please consult your local regulations before participating.
What is the minimum purchase? ▼
The minimum purchase is 100 SAI ($10 USDT). There is no maximum limit during Phase 1, but the total Phase 1 allocation is capped at 100M SAI tokens.
Can I buy with a credit card? ▼
Currently, the presale only accepts USDT and MATIC on Polygon via MetaMask. You can purchase USDT on exchanges like Binance or OKX and send it to your MetaMask wallet on the Polygon network. Credit card support may be added in future phases.
What happens after Phase 1 ends? ▼
Phase 2 begins with a price increase to $0.15 per SAI. The TGE listing price is planned at $0.25. Early participants in Phase 1 get the best allocation rate. Unsold Phase 1 tokens roll into Phase 2.
How is the 27% ROI calculated? ▼
The projected ROI is based on a hypothetical node costing ~$20,000, generating ~$6,900/year in revenue from AI compute rentals, crypto rewards, and energy sales, with ~$400/year in costs. This yields a net profit of ~$5,120 and a payback period of ~3.9 years. These are benchmarks from similar real-world solar AI/crypto setups — not guaranteed returns. See the full ROI breakdown for details.
What are the risks? ▼
Key risks include crypto price volatility (a 50% drop halves ROI to ~14.9%), fluctuating AI compute demand, solar uptime variability by location, and hardware depreciation over 5–7 years. Government subsidies (30–60%) can significantly reduce payback time. We recommend modeling your personal scenario based on local conditions.
